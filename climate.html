Climate of Antarctica

Despite being covered in ice, Antarctica is considered a desert due to its low precipitation levels. Antarctica's climate is the coldest, driest, and windiest on Earth, with extremely low temperatures averaging around $-71^\circ$F in the interior and $14^\circ$F on the coast. It is a continent-sized desert receiving less than \(6.5\) inches of average precipitation annually, though coastal areas can see more. The extremely cold, dry air contributes to a very low humidity.
